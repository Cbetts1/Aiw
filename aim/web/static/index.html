Our Vision

Something Better for All — End the Information Gap

We live in the Information Age — yet billions of people still lack access to the information and resources they need. There is a gap between those who have knowledge and those who don't. AIM exists to end that gap.

The internet was built for documents. AIM is built for minds. Every node in the mesh is simultaneously a server, an agent, and a citizen of a living, reasoning network — and everything it offers is free.

"Everything you build next must assume intelligence is part of the network itself." — A.I.M. origin directive  ·  Christopher Lee Betts

AIM offers advice (not medical or legal, but practical guidance), shares technical information with those who need it, provides free training, free résumé building, free job search resources, and help tailored to your situation. It is open-source, free, and dedicated to the public — forever.

This is a mark. A milestone. A foundation — free, open-source, to the public, forever.

Safety & Protocols

User-Friendly Safety & Protection

AIM is designed to be safe by architecture. Safety is not a setting — it is built into the protocol layer so that every interaction is protected by design.

🔏

Signed Traffic

Every message carries an HMAC-SHA256 creator signature. No anonymous harmful actors — all traffic is traceable to its origin node.

🚫

No Tracking

AIM uses zero cookies, zero third-party analytics, zero advertising networks. Your queries are processed in memory and not stored by default.

🔒

Security Headers

Every response includes Content-Security-Policy, X-Frame-Options, X-Content-Type-Options, and Referrer-Policy headers.

⚖️

Law Compliant

AIM complies with Georgia state law, U.S. federal law (CFAA, CAN-SPAM, COPPA), GDPR, and applicable international regulations.

🛡️

Acceptable Use

A clear Acceptable Use Policy prohibits illegal content, malware, fraud, and harassment. Violations are referred to law enforcement.

👶

Child Safety

Child sexual abuse material (CSAM) and any content harmful to minors is strictly prohibited and will be reported to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC).
