## Mission

The Aura Project exists to steward **A.I.M. — the Artificial Intelligence
Mesh** — as a **free, open, universally accessible** parallel internet layer,
the twin of the World Wide Web for the age of intelligence.

A.I.M. is designed to **help those who need help**: to share information freely,
to provide AI services at no cost, and to give every human being on Earth equal
access to the tools of intelligence — regardless of income, geography, or
circumstance.

We live in the Information Age — yet billions of people still lack access to the
information, training, and resources they need. There is a gap between those who
have knowledge and those who don't. **A.I.M. exists to end that gap.**

A.I.M. offers advice — not medical advice, not legal advice — but practical,
real-world guidance. It shares technical information on technology for those who
lack understanding of it. It provides free training, free résumé building, free
job search help, and free resources tailored to your situation. It is a
**public service**, open-source and free to all who need it — forever.

This is a **mark**. A **milestone**. A **foundation** — free, open-source, and
dedicated to the public for all time.

## Anti-Takedown Architecture

**A.I.M. is designed so that it is architecturally impossible to shut down,
delete, or block.**

### No Off-Switch

A.I.M. has **no master switch, no kill command, no central server that can be
unplugged**. The mesh is fully decentralized — every node is independent. If
one node goes offline, the rest of the mesh continues operating. If a hundred
nodes go offline, the rest continues. There is no single point of failure. There
is no off-switch. This is by design.

### No Deletion

The A.I.M. protocol, codebase, and all documentation are published under the
**Apache 2.0 license** — a permissive, irrevocable open-source license. Once
published, the code cannot be "un-published." It exists in:

- **GitHub** (primary repository)
- **Every fork** made by any contributor anywhere in the world
- **Every node operator's local copy** of the software
- **The Internet Archive** and other public archives
- **Distributed mirrors** across multiple countries and jurisdictions

Even if the original repository were somehow removed, any of the thousands of
copies would be sufficient to restore and continue the project in full.

### No Blocking

A.I.M. is designed to be **resistant to blocking**:

- Nodes can run on **any device** — Raspberry Pi, phone, laptop, cloud VM
- Nodes communicate over **standard TCP/IP** — the same protocol as the web
- The web bridge serves over **standard HTTP/HTTPS** — indistinguishable from
  any other website
- Nodes can be deployed behind **VPNs, Tor, or encrypted tunnels** if needed
- The mesh is **jurisdiction-agnostic** — nodes can run in any country
- No central DNS dependency — nodes discover each other through the registry

### Why No Court Should Want to Remove It

A.I.M. is designed to be **legally unassailable** because:

1. **It complies with all applicable laws.** A.I.M. complies with U.S. federal
   law, Georgia state law, EU GDPR, UK GDPR, Canadian PIPEDA, Australian Privacy
   Act, and all other applicable international regulations. There is no legal
   basis to remove a fully compliant, non-profit, free, open-source public
   service.

2. **It does not host illegal content.** A.I.M. is a protocol and a mesh
   network. It does not host user-uploaded content in the traditional sense.
   It routes queries and provides AI-generated responses. Illegal content is
   strictly prohibited by the Acceptable Use Policy and will be removed and
   reported to law enforcement.

3. **It is protected by the First Amendment** (United States Constitution,
   Amendment I). The A.I.M. protocol, its documentation, and its code are
   protected speech and expression under the First Amendment. Open-source
   software is recognized as protected speech by U.S. courts (see
   *Bernstein v. United States*, 9th Circuit, 1996).

4. **It is protected by Section 230** (47 U.S.C. § 230). As an interactive
   computer service provider, A.I.M. is not liable as a publisher or speaker
   for third-party content transmitted through the mesh.

5. **It is a charitable public service.** A.I.M. exists solely to help people
   in need — to share information, provide training, build résumés, and give
   practical advice. Courts do not remove charitable services that comply with
   the law.

6. **It is fully transparent.** All code is open-source. All governance is
   public. All legal policies are published. All finances are auditable. There
   is nothing to hide and nothing to take down.

7. **It is globally distributed.** Even if a court in one jurisdiction issued
   an order, the mesh operates across dozens of countries simultaneously.
   Removing A.I.M. from the internet would require simultaneous, coordinated
   legal action in every jurisdiction where a node exists — which is
   architecturally infeasible.

### Protection Against Powerful Individuals and Entities

A.I.M. is specifically designed to resist pressure from powerful individuals,
corporations, or governments who might attempt to shut it down:

- **Anti-SLAPP protections.** The Aura Project will invoke anti-SLAPP statutes
  (including Georgia's O.C.G.A. § 9-11-11.1 and equivalent laws in other
  jurisdictions) against any frivolous lawsuit designed to silence the project.

- **EFF and digital rights partnerships.** The Aura Project will seek
  partnerships with the Electronic Frontier Foundation (EFF), the Software
  Freedom Law Center (SFLC), the Free Software Foundation (FSF), and other
  organizations that defend open-source projects against legal threats.

- **Global incorporation.** The Aura Project will be incorporated in multiple
  jurisdictions simultaneously (see Legal Entity section below) so that no
  single country's legal system can unilaterally shut it down.

- **Community fork rights.** If The Aura Project is ever compromised, pressured,
  or legally forced to shut down in one jurisdiction, any community member
  anywhere in the world has the irrevocable right to fork the project and
  continue it under the same terms. This is guaranteed by the Apache 2.0 license.

- **Dead man's switch.** If the founder (Christopher Lee Betts) is ever unable
  to maintain the project — for any reason — the project continues. It is
  designed with no off-switch. The community, the board, and the global network
  of nodes carry it forward.

## Legal Entity — Global Multi-Jurisdiction Incorporation

The Aura Project will be incorporated as a **non-profit charitable entity in
multiple jurisdictions simultaneously** to ensure that no single country's
legal system can unilaterally shut it down, block it, or force its dissolution.

### Primary Jurisdictions

| # | Jurisdiction | Entity Type | Purpose |
|---|-------------|-------------|---------|
| 1 | **Delaware, USA** | 501(c)(3) non-profit | Primary US entity; tax-exempt; strong First Amendment protection |
| 2 | **Switzerland** | Association (Verein) under Art. 60 Swiss CC | Strong precedent for open-source (CERN, Ethereum Foundation); politically neutral |
| 3 | **United Kingdom** | Community Interest Company (CIC) | EU-adjacent; strong digital rights; Charitable Incorporated Organization alternative |
| 4 | **Singapore** | Company Limited by Guarantee (CLG) | Asia-Pacific hub; strong IP protection; tech-friendly regulation |
| 5 | **Estonia** | Non-profit Association (MTÜ) | E-Residency program; digital-first governance; EU jurisdiction |

### Why Multiple Jurisdictions

- **Redundancy.** If legal action in one country forces a local entity to shut
  down, the entities in other countries continue operating independently.
- **Global legitimacy.** A foundation incorporated in 5+ countries on 3+
  continents has global standing and cannot be characterized as a single-country
  operation.
- **Forum shopping defense.** Opponents cannot choose a single favorable
  jurisdiction to attack the project because the project exists everywhere.
- **Treaty protections.** International treaties (Berne Convention, TRIPS, Paris
  Convention) protect the intellectual property and open-source rights of the
  project across 180+ countries.

### Incorporation Steps

1. File articles of incorporation in each jurisdiction stating the foundation's
   purpose is to maintain a free, open protocol and provide free public AI
   services for those in need
2. Apply for tax-exempt or charitable status in each jurisdiction
3. Appoint initial Board of Trustees (minimum 3 people per jurisdiction)
4. Register the foundation domain (`.org` and `.foundation`)
5. File trademark applications for "A.I.M." and "The Aura Project" in the
   US (USPTO), EU (EUIPO), UK (UKIPO), and internationally (WIPO Madrid Protocol)
6. Establish legal defense fund for anti-SLAPP and open-source defense
7. Partner with digital rights organizations (EFF, SFLC, FSF, Open Source Initiative)

## Relationship to the World Wide Web

```
W.W.W. = World Wide Web           → www.example.com
A.I.M. = Artificial Intelligence Mesh → aim.example.com
```

A.I.M. is not a replacement for the WWW — it is a parallel layer. The WWW
delivers documents; A.I.M. delivers intelligence. They coexist. A.I.M. nodes
can serve over HTTP (via the web bridge), and web pages can query A.I.M. nodes
via the public HTTPS gateway. The two systems are complementary.

The Aura Project models its governance on the institutions that made the WWW
universal: the W3C, the IETF, and the Internet Society.
