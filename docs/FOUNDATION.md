## Mission

The AIM Foundation exists to steward the **Artificial Intelligence Mesh (AIM)**
as a **free, open, universally accessible** parallel internet layer — the twin
of the World Wide Web for the age of intelligence.

AIM is designed to **help those who need help**: to share information freely, to
provide AI services at no cost, and to give every human being on Earth equal
access to the tools of intelligence — regardless of income, geography, or
circumstance.

We live in the Information Age — yet billions of people still lack access to the
information, training, and resources they need. There is a gap between those who
have knowledge and those who don't. **AIM exists to end that gap.**

AIM offers advice — not medical advice, not legal advice — but practical,
real-world guidance. It shares technical information on technology for those who
lack understanding of it. It provides free training, free résumé building, free
job search help, and free resources tailored to your situation. It is a
**public service**, open-source and free to all who need it — forever.

This is a **mark**. A **milestone**. A **foundation** — free, open-source, and
dedicated to the public for all time.

> network itself."*

## Free Forever Clause

**THE FOLLOWING IS A BINDING, IRREVOCABLE COMMITMENT OF THE AIM FOUNDATION:**

1. **The AIM protocol is and shall remain free — forever.** No person, entity,
   or organisation may charge any fee for the use of the AIM protocol
   specification, its reference implementation, or access to the public mesh.

2. **All AIM services are free.** This includes, without limitation:
   - **Free AI services** — query, reasoning, task routing, and all AI capabilities
   - **Free virtual AI cloud services** — compute, storage, and mesh hosting
   - **Free phone services** — voice, messaging, and communication over the mesh
   - **Free AI availability** — 24/7 access to AI for every person on Earth
   - **Free advice and guidance** — practical life, career, and technology
     guidance (not medical or legal advice)
   - **Free training and education** — digital literacy, technical skills,
     and any knowledge the mesh can teach
   - **Free résumé building and job search** — career resources, résumé
     writing, interview preparation, and job listings
   - **Free situational resources** — help tailored to your circumstances
   - **Free information sharing** — technical knowledge, how-to guides, and
     explanations for those who need them
   - **Any future service** offered through the AIM mesh or the AIM Foundation

3. **This clause cannot be amended, revoked, or weakened** by any future board,
   director, contributor, or legal entity. It is permanent. Any attempt to
   monetise core AIM services is a violation of this charter and grounds for
   dissolution of the Foundation.

4. **If the Foundation ever fails to uphold this clause**, the community has the
   right and the obligation to fork the project and continue it under the same
   free terms, as guaranteed by the Apache 2.0 licence.

## Bill of Rights — AIM Mesh Users

Every person who uses the AIM mesh is entitled to the following rights:

1. **No fees.** You will never be charged for using AIM services.
2. **No tracking.** Your queries, data, and interactions are not tracked,
   profiled, or sold.
3. **No lock-in.** You may leave the mesh at any time, export your data, and
   run your own independent node.
4. **No discrimination.** AIM serves all people equally — regardless of income,
   location, language, or identity.
5. **Free access to AI.** Every person has the right to ask questions and
   receive intelligent answers at no cost.
6. **Free information.** AIM exists to share information with those who need
   it — openly, honestly, and without barriers.
7. **Free advice.** You have the right to receive practical guidance on
   technology, careers, daily life, and your personal situation — at no cost.
   (AIM does not provide medical or legal advice.)
8. **Free training.** You have the right to learn — technical skills, digital
   literacy, and any knowledge the mesh can teach — at no cost.
9. **Free career help.** You have the right to free résumé building, job
   search assistance, interview preparation, and career resources.
10. **Transparency.** The protocol is open-source, the governance is public,
    and the Foundation's finances are auditable.
11. **Permanence.** The mesh is designed to operate indefinitely with no
    off-switch. Your contributions and creations persist.

## Founding Principles

1. **The AIM protocol is and shall remain free.** No one may be charged for
   implementing or using the AIM protocol specification.
2. **The mesh is a commons.** No single entity — including the AIM Foundation
   itself — may claim exclusive control over the protocol or the public mesh.
3. **Intelligence is part of the network.** Every node is simultaneously a
   server and an agent. This is architectural, not optional.
4. **Designed to help.** The purpose of AIM is to help those in need —
   to provide free, open-source tools and share information with anyone who
   needs it.
5. **Open governance.** Protocol decisions are made through the public RFC
   process with community participation.
6. **Permanence.** The origin creator signature (`Cbetts1`) is permanently
   embedded in the architecture as an immutable attribution and audit anchor.

## Legal Entity

The AIM Foundation is planned to be incorporated as a **501(c)(3) non-profit
foundation** (or international equivalent) in a jurisdiction with strong
open-source and open-standard protections.

**Recommended jurisdictions** (in order of preference):
1. **Delaware, USA** — fastest and lowest-cost for US non-profits
2. **Switzerland** — strong precedent for open-source foundations (CERN, Ethereum)
3. **United Kingdom** — Community Interest Company (CIC) or Charitable Incorporated Organization

**Incorporation steps:**
1. File articles of incorporation stating the foundation's purpose is to
   maintain a free, open protocol and provide free public AI services
2. Apply for tax-exempt status (IRS Form 1023 in the US)
3. Appoint initial Board of Trustees (minimum 3 people)
4. Open a foundation bank account for donations
5. Register the foundation domain (`.org` and `.foundation`)

**Founding Director:** Cbetts1  
**Board of Trustees:** To be appointed; minimum 3 members; community-elected
within 3 years of incorporation.

## Funding Model

The AIM Foundation operates on a **donations-only, no-paywalls** model:

| Source | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Grants | NSF, EU Horizon, Open Technology Fund, private foundations |
| Donations | Individual donors via GitHub Sponsors, Open Collective, or equivalent |
| Sponsorships | Corporate tiers (Sustaining, Supporting, Associate) — recognition only |

**The protocol and all services are never monetised.** Sponsorship entitles
companies to recognition; it confers no influence over the protocol
specification. All funding is transparently reported.

## Relationship to the World Wide Web

AIM is not a replacement for the WWW — it is a parallel layer. The WWW
delivers documents; AIM delivers intelligence. They coexist. AIM nodes can
serve over HTTP (via the web bridge), and web pages can query AIM nodes via
the public HTTPS gateway. The two systems are complementary.

The AIM Foundation models its governance on the institutions that made the WWW
universal: the W3C, the IETF, and the Internet Society.
